# Enterprise Discovery Questions

## Purpose

These questions convert consequence-boundary infrastructure from category language into a bounded enterprise pilot.

The goal is to identify one protected action, one execution boundary, one governing layer, one receipt path, and one acceptance condition.

## Universal discovery questions

1. Which high-risk action creates the most legal, operational, financial, safety, or trust exposure today?
2. Where does that action move from request, recommendation, or approval into actual effect?
3. What system performs the effect?
4. What upstream system requests, approves, or recommends the action?
5. What conditions must hold for the action to remain valid?
6. Which conditions can change after approval but before execution?
7. What should happen when standing is incomplete, stale, contested, or revoked?
8. Who has authority to approve, narrow, escalate, refuse, or halt?
9. Where should receipt and replay evidence be stored?
10. What proof would satisfy legal, audit, security, risk, and business owners?

## Privacy / data movement

1. Which high-risk data action should be protected first?
2. Where does the action become real: disclosure, transfer, sharing, training use, retention, deletion, or downstream processing?
3. Where is lawful basis tracked?
4. Where is consent, objection, or data subject rights state tracked?
5. Where are transfer mechanisms, TIA, adequacy, processor, subprocessor, and DPA status tracked?
6. What actions require DPIA or risk review before execution?
7. What should happen during incident hold or active rights restriction?
8. What reduced/narrowed action is legally acceptable when the full action lacks standing?

## AI output to action

1. Which model output currently influences operational action?
2. Where does output become workflow update, tool call, recommendation, decision, or external effect?
3. Who owns authority to bind model output into action?
4. Which live state must be checked immediately before bind?
5. What outputs should be narrowed, escalated, refused, or halted?
6. What replay evidence is required to prove why the output was or was not allowed to bind?

## Aerospace / engineering stage admission

1. Which stage transition should be protected first?
2. What formation moves from concept to simulation, controlled review, or certification packet?
3. What safety, witness, monotonicity, integrity, and replay conditions must hold?
4. What constitutes non-monotone advancement?
5. What should return to prior stage, halt, quarantine, or certify bounded proof only?
6. Who supplies the witness or review authority?

## Privileged operations

1. Which privileged operation creates the most exposure?
2. Where does authentication end and execution begin?
3. What live authority, revocation, risk, context, or continuation state must hold?
4. Which operations may be narrowed rather than refused?
5. What emergency or break-glass paths exist?
6. How should receipts bind to existing security/audit systems?

## AI memory / identity / agent continuity

1. Which memory, identity, intent, or agency formation should be protected first?
2. What does it mean for that formation to bind?
3. What continuity, coherence, standing, replay, contamination, and target-legitimacy checks must hold?
4. Which formations may be observed without binding?
5. Which should rebound, refuse, quarantine, or halt?
6. What proof is needed to show the formation was admitted or blocked correctly?

## Pilot close question

```text
Which one protected action class can we evaluate first, with a clear boundary, clear standing inputs, clear outcome posture, and receipt/replay evidence?
```
